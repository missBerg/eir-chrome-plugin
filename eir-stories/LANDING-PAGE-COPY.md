## Section: Why It Exists

Getting access to your health records is only the first step.

Most people still face the same problems:
- their records are fragmented
- the journey is hard to read
- they feel alone in what they are going through

Eir Stories exists to turn health data into something more human: a story someone else can understand.

## Section: How It Works

### 1. Start from your own records

Use data you already accessed through Eir or upload records you control yourself.

### 2. Build your journey

Turn diagnoses, visits, medications, procedures, and outcomes into a readable timeline.

### 3. Review for privacy

The platform helps you remove names, IDs, exact locations, and other identifying details before publishing.

### 4. Publish what you choose

Share a public version of your health journey only if and when you want to.

## Section: Why Someone Would Share

People share for different reasons:
- to help others feel less alone
- to explain what a real treatment path looked like
- to document what worked and what did not
- to make sense of their own journey

## Section: Why Someone Would Read

People come to Eir Stories to find:
- someone with the same diagnosis
- someone with similar symptoms
- someone who has tried the same treatment
- a clearer idea of what a health journey can actually look like

## Section: Privacy

Eir Stories is built for careful sharing, not oversharing.

The platform should make it clear that:
- publishing is optional
- direct identifiers should be removed
- private source records are not the same thing as public story pages
- users review everything before it goes live
